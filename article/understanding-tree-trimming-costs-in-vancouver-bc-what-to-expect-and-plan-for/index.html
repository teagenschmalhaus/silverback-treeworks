Understanding Tree Trimming Costs in Vancouver, BC: What to Expect and Plan For

Tree Pruning Vancouver, BC Silverback Treeworks

Tree trimming costs in Vancouver vary widely — a small ornamental cherry and a 25-metre Douglas fir are completely different jobs. This guide breaks down what actually drives pricing so you know what to expect when you get a quote.

Average Tree Trimming Costs in Vancouver

Tree SizeTypical HeightEstimated Cost (CAD)
SmallUp to 6 m$200–$400
Medium6–12 m$400–$800
Large12–18 m$800–$1,600
Very LargeOver 18 m$1,600–$3,200+

ISA Certified Arborists typically charge $150–$300 per hour. Adding debris removal and stump grinding can increase the total by $250–$800.

What Factors Drive the Cost?

Tree Size and Height

Larger trees require more crew time, specialized equipment, and more complex safety planning. Working high in the canopy often means aerial lifts or advanced rope-access techniques — both of which increase time on site.

Tree Species and Wood Hardness

Hardwood trees like oaks and maples require stronger equipment and more experienced crews. Diseased or decaying trees need special handling. Softwood species like pine and cedar may trim faster but create significant debris.

Location and Accessibility

Trees near structures require protective measures and careful branch lowering. Power line proximity requires utility coordination. Steep terrain — common in North Vancouver and the Sea to Sky — increases manual labour requirements and crane time.

Type of Pruning Work

  • Pruning and shaping: trimming smaller branches for appearance and guided growth
  • Crown reduction: lowering canopy height while preserving natural form
  • Deadwood removal: eliminating dead limbs for safety
  • Crown thinning: selective removal to reduce wind resistance and improve light

When Do You Need a Permit for Tree Trimming in Vancouver?

For minor pruning that doesn't affect a tree's health or remove major limbs, permits are generally not required. However, trees measuring over 20 cm in diameter (measured at 1.4 metres from the ground) are protected under Vancouver's tree bylaw — any significant work on them should be done by a certified arborist who understands the city's requirements.

Does Timing Affect Cost?

Emergency services typically cost 30–50% more than scheduled work. Scheduling in the off-peak season (late fall to early winter) can provide cost advantages and better scheduling flexibility. Arborists often group jobs during these slower months, which can mean preferred appointment times and occasional discounts.

How to Get an Accurate Quote

When contacting Silverback Treeworks for a quote, it helps to have the following ready:

  • Tree species, approximate trunk diameter, and height
  • Photos showing the canopy density and access to the tree
  • What work you want done (pruning, crown reduction, deadwooding)
  • Any concerns about nearby structures, power lines, or underground utilities
