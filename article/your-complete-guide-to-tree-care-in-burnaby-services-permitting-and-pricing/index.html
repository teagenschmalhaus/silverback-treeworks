Your Complete Guide to Tree Care in Burnaby: Services, Permitting & Pricing

Tree Removal Burnaby, BC Silverback Treeworks

Burnaby's mature residential canopy — particularly on Burnaby Mountain, South Burnaby, and Deer Lake neighbourhoods — means homeowners regularly deal with large, established trees. This guide covers the common reasons for tree work, Burnaby's permit requirements, and what professional tree care typically costs.

Why Tree Care Matters in Burnaby

  • Dead or hazardous trees: pose liability risks during storms and high winds
  • Overgrown branches: can threaten power lines, roofs, or fences
  • Disease or pest infestations: require early intervention to prevent spread
  • Storm damage: requires immediate attention for safety
  • Property development: clearing space for construction or landscaping projects

Do You Need a Permit for Tree Removal in Burnaby?

The City of Burnaby requires a Tree Cutting Permit for any tree greater than 20 cm (8 inches) in diameter, measured 1.4 metres above the ground. Applications typically require site plans, photographs, diameter measurements, and in some cases an arborist report.

When a Permit IS Required

  • Private property trees 20 cm or more in diameter
  • Protected tree species
  • Trees in environmentally sensitive areas
  • Trees on public property

When a Permit Is NOT Required

  • Trees under 20 cm diameter
  • Hazardous, dead, or structurally unstable trees — but documentation from a certified arborist is still required

Silverback Treeworks handles the permit process for our Burnaby clients from start to finish.

Tree Removal Costs in Burnaby

Our day rate for a three-person crew with truck and chipper is approximately $3,000. Most residential jobs complete in a single day. Spider lift services add approximately $600 when a lift is required for access.

Key cost drivers:

  • Tree size and height: larger trees require more crew and equipment time
  • Access: tight yards, steep slopes (common in Burnaby), or proximity to structures
  • Condition: diseased or dead trees require more careful handling
  • Stump grinding: typically $150–$500 depending on stump diameter

Professional Tree Removal: What to Expect

  1. Thorough assessment by a certified arborist — on site, not over the phone
  2. Permit application handled on your behalf if required
  3. Safety-first approach using appropriate rigging and sectional removal
  4. Site cleanup and debris disposal included in the quote
  5. Optional stump grinding as an add-on
