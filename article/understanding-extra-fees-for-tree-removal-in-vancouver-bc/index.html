Understanding Extra Fees for Tree Removal in Vancouver, BC

Tree Removal Vancouver, BC Silverback Treeworks

Tree removal quotes sometimes come in higher than expected — not because the company is padding the bill, but because large or complex jobs have real additional costs. This guide explains exactly what drives those extra charges so you're not caught off guard.

Permit and Report Fees

Vancouver's Protection of Trees Bylaw 9958 requires permits for trees 20 cm or larger in diameter. Permit costs:

  • First tree: $108
  • Each additional tree (within 12 months): $310
  • Arborist report: $150–$300 (required for most permit applications)

The District of North Vancouver charges $100 for up to four large-diameter trees, plus environmental compensation fees of $1,000–$2,000 for required replanting. Unauthorized removal can result in fines from $1,000 to $20,000 per tree.

Debris Removal and Hauling

Debris removal and hauling typically costs $100–$250 per load. Most Silverback Treeworks quotes include chipping and hauling of all debris — confirm this when you get your quote so there are no surprises.

Common Unexpected Charges

  • Arborist redesign charges when on-site conditions differ from what was quoted
  • Crew overtime on jobs that run long due to access complications
  • Additional debris storage costs on multi-day jobs
  • Emergency surcharges for after-hours or storm-response work (typically 30–50% above standard rates)

How to Avoid Surprises

The best protection against unexpected charges is a detailed, written quote from a certified arborist who has physically assessed the site. Phone or email quotes based on descriptions frequently miss site-specific complications. We provide free on-site assessments with itemized written estimates.
