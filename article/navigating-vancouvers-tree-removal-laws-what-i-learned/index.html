Navigating Vancouver's Tree Removal Laws — What I Learned

Bylaws & Permits Vancouver, BC Silverback Treeworks

Why Vancouver Has Strict Tree Removal Rules

Vancouver enforces some of the most comprehensive urban tree protections in Canada. The city's goal is to increase canopy cover from 18% to 30% by 2030 — and the Protection of Trees Bylaw is the primary tool for getting there. Trees provide stormwater management, air quality improvement, urban cooling, and wildlife habitat. The bylaw protects that investment.

For homeowners, this means you can't simply cut down a tree on your own property without understanding whether it's protected. Getting it wrong can result in significant fines.

Protection of Trees Bylaw No. 9958

Vancouver's Protection of Trees Bylaw 9958 governs the removal, protection, and replacement of trees on private land. It applies to any tree with a trunk diameter of 20 cm or more, measured 1.4 metres above the ground. If your tree meets that threshold, you need a permit before it can be removed.

The bylaw also requires an arborist assessment and a replacement planting plan in most cases.

When Do You Need a Permit?

A permit is required when:

  • The tree trunk is 20 cm or larger in diameter at 1.4 m above ground
  • The tree is a heritage or exceptional specimen, regardless of size
  • The tree is in a protected greenway or environmentally sensitive area

Permits are generally not required for:

  • Trees under 20 cm in diameter
  • Hazard removals where there is imminent risk to life or property (though you must notify the city within 48 hours)
  • Trees that were planted within the last two years

How to Apply: The 5-Step Process

  1. Hire an ISA Certified Arborist to assess the tree and prepare a report
  2. Obtain site plans and photographs of the tree and its surroundings
  3. Complete the City of Vancouver's online application form
  4. Submit documentation with the required fees
  5. Wait for approval — typically 10–21 business days

Penalties for Unauthorized Removal

Removing a protected tree without a permit is treated seriously by the City of Vancouver:

  • First offence: up to $1,000 per tree
  • Subsequent offences: up to $5,000 per tree
  • Serious violations: up to $20,000 per tree

In addition to fines, you may be required to replant replacement trees at your own expense.
