North Vancouver Tree Removal: The Ultimate Guide to Costs, Permits & Expert Advice

Tree Removal North Vancouver, BC Silverback Treeworks

Whether you're dealing with a dead Douglas fir on a steep slope or a mature cedar too close to your foundation, tree removal in North Vancouver requires some planning. This guide covers everything homeowners and property managers need to know — permit thresholds for both the City and District of North Vancouver, typical costs, and what a professional removal actually looks like from start to finish.

Why Tree Removal in North Vancouver Is Sometimes Necessary

  • Dead or dying trees that pose a safety hazard during windstorms
  • Storm-damaged trees with major structural failure
  • Overgrown roots threatening foundations, driveways, or underground pipes
  • Pest and disease spread that can affect neighbouring trees
  • Canopy obstructing power lines or creating excessive shade

Do You Need a Permit for Tree Removal in North Vancouver?

North Vancouver has two separate jurisdictions with different rules. Make sure you know which one your property falls under.

City of North Vancouver

A permit is required when a tree has a trunk diameter exceeding 20 cm at breast height (measured 1.4 m above ground), is within 15 metres of a watercourse or 10 metres of a ravine bank, or is a heritage-designated or protected species.

District of North Vancouver

The District has stricter size thresholds and broader protected species coverage. Permits are required for:

  • Trees with a trunk diameter over 75 cm (in some cases, 20 cm applies)
  • Trees on slopes of 20% or greater
  • Protected species: Arbutus, Garry Oak, Oregon Ash, Pacific Yew, Western White Pine, and Yellow-Cedar
  • Trees within Streamside Protection and Enhancement Areas (SPEAs)

What Does Tree Removal Cost in North Vancouver?

Costs vary significantly based on tree size, proximity to structures, condition, and whether stump grinding is included. Key cost factors:

  • Tree size: larger diameter and height means more crew time and equipment
  • Access: steep slopes and tight yards in North Vancouver frequently require crane work
  • Condition: dead or structurally compromised trees require more careful handling
  • Stump grinding: typically an add-on, charged by stump diameter

We recommend getting a free on-site quote — photographs don't capture the full picture for complex North Vancouver jobs. Our day rate for a three-person crew with truck and chipper is approximately $3,000, with most residential jobs completing in a single day.

The Tree Removal Process: What to Expect

  1. Assessment: an ISA Certified Arborist visits the site, assesses the tree, and documents what's needed for the permit application
  2. Permit handling: we prepare and submit the arborist report and application on your behalf
  3. Safety planning: crew plans the cut sequence, rigging points, and drop zones before any chainsaw starts
  4. Removal: tree is sectioned and lowered in a controlled sequence
  5. Cleanup: all debris is chipped or hauled; stump ground if requested
