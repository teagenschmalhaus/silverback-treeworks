Common Questions

Hedge Trimming FAQ

How often should hedges be trimmed?

Most hedges in BC benefit from trimming twice a year — once in late spring after the main flush of growth, and again in late summer. Fast-growing species like cherry laurel may need three cuts. Slower species like boxwood or cedar are typically fine with one annual trim.

Can you cut back a hedge that's been neglected for years?

Yes, in most cases. Many hedge species tolerate hard rejuvenation cuts and will respond with vigorous new growth. However, some conifers (like cedar) won't regenerate from old wood, so the approach depends on the species. We'll assess your hedge and recommend the best strategy during your free estimate.

What types of hedges do you trim?

We trim all common hedge species found in Greater Vancouver including cedar, Western red cedar, English and cherry laurel, boxwood, privet, photinia, holly, yew, hornbeam, and more. We also handle ornamental shrubs — rhododendrons, azaleas, camellias, spiraea, and similar plants.
