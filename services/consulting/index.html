Common Questions

Consulting FAQ

When do I need a formal arborist report?

Most municipalities in Metro Vancouver require an arborist report to remove trees above a certain size on private property — typically 20–30 cm diameter at breast height (DBH), depending on the city. You may also need one if you're doing construction near significant trees, applying for a development permit, or dealing with an insurance or legal dispute involving trees. When in doubt, call your city hall or contact us — we're familiar with the bylaws across all our service areas.

How long does it take to get a report?

After a site visit, most reports are completed within 5–10 business days. Urgent requests can sometimes be accommodated faster — let us know your timeline when you get in touch.

Can a report help me save a tree the city wants removed?

Sometimes, yes. A well-prepared arborist report can demonstrate that a tree is in better condition than initially assessed, support a variance application, or outline a management plan that satisfies permit requirements without full removal. We always aim to find retention solutions where they're genuinely viable.
