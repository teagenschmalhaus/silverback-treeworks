Our Services

Tree Pruning

Proper pruning is one of the most important things you can do for your trees. Done right, it improves structure, reduces risk, and extends the life of the tree. Done wrong — especially topping — it causes lasting damage. Silverback's ISA Certified Arborists prune to industry standards, removing only what needs to go and always with the tree's long-term health in mind.

We prune everything from young street trees being trained for good structure to mature Douglas firs needing crown reduction and deadwood removal. Residential, strata, commercial — we handle it all.

Common Questions

Tree Pruning FAQ

When is the best time to prune trees?

For most species in BC, late fall through early spring (when trees are dormant) is ideal. Pruning during dormancy minimises stress, reduces disease transmission risk, and allows wounds to seal before the growing season. That said, hazardous deadwood and structural problems should be addressed any time of year — don't wait if there's a safety concern.

What's the difference between topping and crown reduction?

Topping is the indiscriminate removal of large branches, leaving stubs and destroying the tree's natural form. It causes decay, watersprout growth, and often kills the tree within years. Crown reduction, by contrast, makes cuts back to lateral branches, maintains the tree's structure, and achieves size reduction without the damaging side effects. We never top trees.

How often should trees be pruned?

It depends on the species, age, and location. Young trees benefit from structural pruning every 3–5 years to develop good form. Mature trees typically need maintenance pruning every 5–7 years. Trees near structures, power lines, or with known health issues may need more frequent attention. We'll recommend a schedule based on your specific trees.
