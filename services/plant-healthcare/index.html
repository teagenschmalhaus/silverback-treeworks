Plant Healthcare

Removing and pruning trees is reactive. Plant healthcare is proactive — keeping your trees strong, resilient, and valuable for decades. Silverback's ISA Certified Arborists identify underlying health issues and develop treatment programs tailored to your specific trees and soil conditions.

Urban trees in Greater Vancouver face constant stressors: compacted soil, drought, root competition, pests, and disease. A targeted plant healthcare program addresses these at the root level — literally — so trees don't just survive, they thrive.

Common Questions

Plant Healthcare FAQ

How do I know if my tree needs a healthcare program?

Signs that a tree may be stressed include thinning canopy, smaller-than-normal leaves, early fall colour, dieback at branch tips, bark abnormalities, or visible pests and fungi. Many of these symptoms appear 2–3 years after the initial stress event, so early intervention is key. If you're unsure, our arborists can assess your trees and advise on whether intervention is warranted.

What pests affect trees in BC?

Common pests in Greater Vancouver include Western tent caterpillars, aphids, spider mites, bronze birch borer, emerald ash borer (an emerging concern), pine bark beetles, and various scale insects. Many are manageable with targeted treatment when caught early. Our arborists can identify the specific pest and recommend the appropriate response.

How long does it take to see results from fertilization?

Most trees show visible improvement — denser foliage, improved colour, new growth — within one full growing season following treatment. Trees under significant stress may take 2–3 seasons to fully respond. Deep-root fertilization is most effective when combined with soil aeration and other cultural practices that address the root cause of decline.
