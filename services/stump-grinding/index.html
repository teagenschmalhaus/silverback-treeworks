Our Services

Stump Grinding

A leftover stump is a tripping hazard, a magnet for pests, and an eyesore. Silverback's stump grinding service removes stumps completely — ground down below grade so the area can be filled, seeded, or replanted. We handle stumps of any size, including large root flares and multiple stumps in a single visit.

Our equipment can access tight backyards and sloped properties that other companies can't reach. The result is a clean, level surface with wood chip mulch left in place to decompose naturally — or hauled away if you prefer.

Common Questions

Stump Grinding FAQ

How deep do you grind the stump?

Typically 8–12 inches below grade, which is sufficient for filling, sodding, or planting. If you're planning to pour concrete or lay pavers over the area, let us know and we can grind deeper or advise on the best approach.

Will the stump or roots grow back?

Grinding destroys the stump and prevents regrowth from the main trunk. Some species (like poplar and cherry) can send up suckers from remaining roots — if this is a concern, we can discuss chemical root treatment options after grinding.

Can I plant a new tree where the stump was?

Yes, but we recommend waiting 6–12 months for the remaining wood chips and root material to decompose before planting in the same spot. Alternatively, plant slightly offset from the old stump location. We can advise on replanting during your estimate.

What happens to the wood chips after grinding?

You can keep them as mulch — they're great for garden beds and decompose into the soil over 1–2 years. If you'd prefer the area left clean, we can haul the chips away for an additional fee. Just let us know your preference when you book.
